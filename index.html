Virginia Medicaid & FAMIS
Free health insurance enrollment · CoverVA.org
FREE / Low cost

You may qualify for free or very low-cost insurance. Virginia expanded Medicaid — adults earning up to 138% of the federal poverty level (~$20,783/year individually) qualify. FAMIS covers uninsured children under 19. Apply online in minutes.

📱 Apply online anytime at CoverVA.gov
Apply now
Medicaid eligibility

Adults 19–64 earning ≤138% FPL (~$20,783/yr single, ~$35,632/yr family of 3). Pregnant women, children, and people with disabilities may have higher income limits.

What's covered

Doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, mental health, dental (limited), and vision. No monthly premium for most Medicaid enrollees.

How to apply

Online at CoverVA.org — takes about 15 minutes. You can also apply in person at the Richmond Social Services office or call 1-800-552-3431.

Prescription Assistance Programs
NeedyMeds · GoodRx · Manufacturer PAPs
FREE / Discounted

Many prescriptions are available free or at deep discounts. NeedyMeds.org lists manufacturer Patient Assistance Programs (PAPs) that give brand-name drugs free to low-income patients. GoodRx cards cut pharmacy costs up to 80% — no insurance needed at all.

🔍 Search by drug name or condition
GoodRx NeedyMeds
How NeedyMeds works

Search your medication → apply directly to the drug manufacturer → most PAPs mail medication to your doctor's office or directly to you. Free for qualifying patients.

How GoodRx works

Show the free GoodRx card or app at any pharmacy (CVS, Walgreens, Kroger, etc.). No enrollment required. Works even with insurance if GoodRx is cheaper.

Good to know before your visit

No insurance? That's OK.

All clinics listed here serve patients regardless of insurance status. Sliding fee scales mean you pay based on income — often $0 to $20 per visit.

You may qualify for Medicaid.

Virginia expanded coverage — earn under ~$20,783/yr? You likely qualify for free insurance. Apply in 15 min at CoverVA.org.

What to bring to your visit.

Photo ID, list of current medications, and immunization records if available. Free clinics don't require insurance cards or proof of income.
